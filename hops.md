# Climate Change and Hop Production

This project explored whether climate change is affecting hop production in Yakima, Washington — the largest hop‑producing region in the U.S.

---

## What I Did

- Collected temperature and precipitation data from NOAA  
- Collected hop production data from USDA  
- Cleaned and merged datasets  
- Visualized 24 years of climate and production trends  
- Compared hop yields to temperature and precipitation  

---

## Key Findings

- Temperatures in Yakima have increased slightly  
- Precipitation has decreased slightly  
- Hop production does not appear to be negatively affected  
- Yield changes are more likely tied to market demand than climate  

---

## Why This Project Matters

Climate change is a major concern for agriculture. This analysis shows how data can be used to validate or challenge assumptions about environmental impact.
