My experience comes from building automation and HVAC systems, but over time I’ve gotten more interested in data, simulation, and the ways those tools can help solve real operational problems.

I’m not locked into one specialty. I like learning new things, trying out ideas, and building projects that teach me something useful. Sometimes that means digging into Python, sometimes it means troubleshooting equipment, and sometimes it means experimenting with data science concepts to see how they apply to the real world.

One of the bigger projects I’m working on right now is SIM‑1, a digital‑twin model of the facility I manage. It started small and grew into something more complex, but that’s pretty normal for me — most of my projects evolve as I learn more and see what’s possible. SIM‑1 is just one example of the kind of work I enjoy: hands‑on, technical, and grounded in real systems.

Outside of that, I’m always working on smaller projects, school assignments, or anything that helps me build skills in analytics, programming, and system modeling. I like practical work that connects back to real problems, whether that’s improving equipment behavior, understanding data patterns, or building tools that make life easier.

This portfolio is a place to collect the things I’m working on and show the range of what I do — not just one project, but the overall direction I’m growing in.
